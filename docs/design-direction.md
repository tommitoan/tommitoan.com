# Design Direction

## Experience target

The site should feel like entering a personal command deck or curated digital habitat.

It should not feel like:

- a template portfolio
- a SaaS landing page
- a generic dev portfolio with neon gradients everywhere

## Visual direction

Keep the strongest parts of `toanngo.cv`:

- cinematic atmosphere
- starfield and spatial depth
- intentional motion
- premium, editorial feeling

Evolve it for `tommitoan.com` by making it more architectural and portal-like.

## Mood keywords

- cinematic
- atmospheric
- engineered
- exploratory
- personal
- futuristic but grounded

## Panel concept

The 3 entry panels should feel like 3 destinations, not simple cards.

Each panel should have:

- distinct iconography or environment cue
- a clear label
- a one-line promise
- motion response on hover or focus
- a strong transition when selected

Suggested moods:

- `Portfolio` - sharp, minimal, polished, recruiter-friendly
- `Discover` - immersive, exploratory, world-building
- `Products` - operational, tool-oriented, active systems

## Motion direction

Use fewer, more meaningful motions:

- homepage intro reveal
- panel hover depth shift
- panel selection collapse and expansion
- staggered content reveals on deeper pages

Avoid:

- overusing floating animations
- too many small micro-interactions
- constant movement that weakens readability

## Typography

Avoid default corporate feel.

Recommendation:

- headline font with more character
- clean readable body font
- strong contrast between display and body typography

The system should still prioritize readability on mobile.

## Color direction

Do not blindly reuse purple-heavy defaults.

Better direction:

- deep midnight or graphite base
- cold metal, blue, cyan, and controlled amber highlights
- use violet sparingly as a supporting accent instead of the main identity

## Background system

Use layered depth:

- starfield or dust field
- soft gradients
- structural glows
- optional geometric grid or signal lines

Avoid flat backgrounds.

## Mobile behavior

On mobile, the panel experience should remain strong:

- stack panels vertically
- preserve identity and contrast
- keep transitions lightweight
- do not hide the concept behind overly complex motion

## Accessibility guardrails

- strong text contrast
- visible keyboard focus states
- motion reduced when users prefer reduced motion
- panel transitions must remain usable without hover
