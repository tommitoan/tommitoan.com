# Business Strategy

## Product role

`tommitoan.com` is not just a portfolio. It is the public gateway to your personal brand ecosystem.

Its business job is to turn scattered identity signals into one clear front door.

## Core business outcomes

### Outcome 1 - Brand clarity

Visitors should quickly understand:

- who you are
- what kind of builder you are
- what makes your ecosystem different from a normal engineer portfolio

### Outcome 2 - Better routing

Different audiences should reach the right destination fast:

- recruiters go to `Portfolio`
- curious technical visitors go to `Discover`
- users interested in live tools go to `Products`

### Outcome 3 - Trust building

The site should make your work feel real, active, and current through:

- live products
- homelab story
- public content
- selected self-hosted proof points

## Primary audiences

### Recruiters and hiring managers

Need:

- fast proof of professional credibility
- low-friction resume path
- confidence that you are current and serious

Primary route:

- `Portfolio`

### Engineering peers

Need:

- technical depth
- systems thinking
- real-world infra and product taste

Primary route:

- `Discover`

### Product users and curious visitors

Need:

- easy access to useful public things you built
- clear status and trust signals

Primary route:

- `Products`

## Positioning

Suggested positioning statement:

`tommitoan.com` is the gateway to Toan Ngo's work across software engineering, cloud infrastructure, self-hosting, experiments, and public products.

Shorter external phrasing:

`Builder of backend systems, self-hosted platforms, and useful digital products.`

## Why `Products` is the right label

- it signals output, not just skill
- it supports tools, demos, apps, and experiments
- it avoids sounding like generic freelance consulting
- it leaves room to add a separate `Work With Me` page later if business needs change

## Business guardrails

- do not overload the homepage with too many offers
- do not expose private or unfinished tools just because they exist
- do not duplicate `toanngo.cv`
- do not make the site feel like a tool directory before trust is established

## Phase 1 value pillars

- `Identity` - strong personal brand entry point
- `Proof` - visible technical depth through homelab and products
- `Routing` - clear audience navigation

## Phase 2 expansion options

- `Work With Me` page if you want consulting or advisory positioning
- blog or writing hub
- product waitlists or launch pages
- newsletter or update feed
- selective private-login surfaces for yourself

## Success signals

Early qualitative success:

- people understand the difference between `tommitoan.com` and `toanngo.cv`
- visitors can describe the 3 paths without explanation
- the site feels more memorable than a normal dev portfolio

Later measurable signals:

- clicks from homepage to `Portfolio`
- clicks from homepage to `Discover`
- clicks from homepage to `Products`
- time spent on `Discover`
- outbound clicks to Bazica and future product pages

## Recommended phase order from a business perspective

1. establish the gateway and message
2. make `Discover` feel alive and personal
3. make `Products` useful and trustworthy
4. later monetize or deepen content only after the identity is clear
